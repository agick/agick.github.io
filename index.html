Lets dig at bit deeper...

Looking at everything that has been said during the meetings at the parliment, we want to find out more about how they use language.

We will start by looking at the sentiment of the parties.

The sentiment score is a relative number where we have taken into account the total amount of text for each party. This allows to better compare the parties

Looking at the data we can see that the parties with the most extreme political views (both left and right leaning), also ranks lowest in sentiment score. Coincident? We don't think so.

We will now look into the lix number, which is a number indicating readability

The lix analysis shows that all parties use language with medium readability. We can also see that the two parties ranking lowest are parties based in greenland, which might explain the lower score.

The politicians

We will start by looking at how the politicians refer to each other. This scatterplot shows the distribution of ingoing and outgoing links. Every datapoint is a politician.

From the scatterplot we can conclude that a few politicians are mentioned often and that these politicians also seems to mention a lot of politicians themself

Lets see which politicians are mentioned most often (ingoing mentions). And who mentions other politicians the most (outgoing mentions). Notice that the colors of the bars represent what party the person is from.

From these graphs we can see that the politicians that are right leaning (members of V,DF,NB,KF) have a lot more ingoing and outgoing mentions. And that Morten Messerschmidt has the most ingoing links and the second most outgoing links. Everything revolves around him apparently.

We are also interested in seeing how agreeable the politicians are. We created a graph that links the politicians when they vote them same. The color of the nodes are the party they belong to.
